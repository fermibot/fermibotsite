üî¨ About Cellular Respiration

Cellular respiration is the set of metabolic reactions that convert biochemical energy from nutrients into adenosine triphosphate (ATP), the universal energy currency of cells. This complex process involves multiple interconnected pathways that work together to extract maximum energy from glucose molecules while managing byproducts and maintaining metabolic balance.

The visualization below shows the intricate network of metabolic pathways, enzymes, and intermediates involved in this process. Each connection represents a biochemical relationship, revealing how different pathways interact and depend on one another.

üìä Metabolic Pathways Visualized
  • Glycolysis - Breakdown of glucose to pyruvate (produces 2 ATP + 2 NADH)
  • Anaerobic Pathways - Fermentation processes when oxygen is limited
  • Cori Cycle - Lactate recycling between muscle and liver tissues
  • Krebs Cycle | Stage 01 - Citric acid cycle preparation phase
  • Krebs Cycle | Stage 02 - Oxidative decarboxylation and energy harvest
  • Hormonal Regulation - Insulin, glucagon, and metabolic control
  • Glycogen Metabolism - Energy storage (glycogenesis) and mobilization (glycogenolysis)
  • Gluconeogenesis - Glucose synthesis from lactate, amino acids, and glycerol
  • Pentose Phosphate Pathway - NADPH production for biosynthesis and ribose-5-phosphate for nucleotides
