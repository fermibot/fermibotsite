üí° What is Zipf's Law?

Zipf's Law states that in natural language, the frequency of any word is inversely proportional to its rank in the frequency table. In other words, the most frequent word occurs approximately twice as often as the second most frequent word, three times as often as the third most frequent word, and so on.
